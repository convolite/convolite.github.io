Is Abortion Wrong?

The topic of abortion is one of the most debated and divisive issues of our time. It's a subject that raises strong emotions and sparks intense discussions. As we explore this complex issue, it's essential to approach it with compassion, empathy, and a deep understanding of the value of human life. From a biblical perspective, the sanctity of human life is a fundamental principle that shapes our understanding of abortion and its implications.

The Value of Human Life in the Bible

The Bible teaches that human life is precious and sacred. In Psalm 139:13-14, David writes, "For you created my inmost being; you knit me together in my mother's womb. I praise you because I am fearfully and wonderfully made; your works are wonderful, I know that full well." This passage emphasizes the remarkable and intricate process of human development, highlighting the unique and special nature of every individual.

In Genesis 1:27, we read, "So God created mankind in his own image, in the image of God he created them; male and female he created them." This verse underscores the inherent value and dignity of human life, as we are created in the image of God. This fundamental principle is echoed throughout the Bible, emphasizing the importance of respecting and protecting human life.

The Unborn Child: A Human Being with Rights

The question of when life begins is a crucial aspect of the abortion debate. From a biblical perspective, life begins at conception. In Psalm 51:5, David writes, "Surely I was sinful at birth, sinful from the time my mother conceived me." This passage suggests that David recognized his own sinfulness from the moment of conception, implying that he was a human being with a moral nature from the very beginning.

In Jeremiah 1:5, God says, "Before I formed you in the womb I knew you, before you were born I set you apart; I appointed you as a prophet to the nations." This verse highlights God's intimate knowledge and involvement in the life of the unborn child, emphasizing the child's inherent value and purpose.

The Consequences of Abortion

Abortion has far-reaching consequences that affect not only the unborn child but also the mother, the family, and society as a whole. The emotional and psychological impact of abortion can be devastating, leading to feelings of guilt, shame, and regret.

In addition to the personal consequences, abortion also has significant societal implications. The devaluation of human life can lead to a culture of disposability, where the vulnerable and marginalized are seen as less worthy of protection and care.

Alternatives to Abortion

While abortion may seem like a solution to an unplanned pregnancy, there are alternative options available. Adoption, for example, provides a loving and supportive environment for the child, while also giving the mother the opportunity to make a positive choice.

Pregnancy resource centers and crisis pregnancy centers offer vital support and resources for women facing unplanned pregnancies. These organizations provide counseling, medical care, and practical assistance, empowering women to make informed decisions about their pregnancies.

Conclusion

The sanctity of human life is a fundamental principle that underlies our understanding of abortion. From a biblical perspective, human life is precious and sac, created in the image of God. The unborn child is a human being with inherent value and rights, deserving of our protection and care.

As we navigate the complex issue of abortion, let us remember the importance of compassion, empathy, and understanding. Let us work together to create a culture that values and respects human life, from conception to natural death.

When Jesus receives you, you are united with him. He goes to God on your behalf. And do you know what our mighty Father God says? “You have been crucified with Christ — you’ve already been through judgment — you are now hidden in Jesus. You are my child in whom I am well pleased.” You are fully known and loved by God.

And the beautiful part? Your child is waiting for you. He or she is with Jesus, and when she asks when she will get to see you, Jesus simply replies, “Tomorrow,” because one day in his courts is 1,000 years on this earth.

If you have had an abortion, find a friend you can trust and open up about your story. Look for a biblical counselor and join an abortion recovery group if there is one in your area. You are not alone, and someday God could use your story to free another woman. Who the Son sets free is free indeed.
